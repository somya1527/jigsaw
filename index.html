HAPPY AANNIVVEERRSAARRYY DOBBII!!!!!!❤

Seven years bhai… seven years is a long time by any standard, long enough for people to change cities, careers, friend circles, even personalities. And yet, somehow, through all the distance, the silence, endless convos, the misunderstandings, the stupid fights, the laughter, and everything in between… we’re still here.

And when I think about where it all started, I think about that simple, ordinary place, our tuition. The big and small muddas, the sound of pages flipping, Sapna maam explaining things we barely cared about, and somewhere in that completely normal setting… something quietly extraordinary began.

I didn’t know it then. I don’t think you did either.

At first, you were just… you. Someone I noticed, someone who slowly became familiar. There wasn’t a single moment where everything suddenly changed. It was subtle, like a feeling growing in the background without announcing itself.

And then one day, it wasn’t just “someone from tuition” anymore.

It was you.

I still smile when I think about those early days. The small conversations that felt bigger than they should have. The unnecessary reasons to talk. The way time somehow moved faster when you were around. And the way I would replay those moments in my head later, trying to understand why they mattered so much.

Maybe that’s the thing about us, we never needed something dramatic to begin. We just… happened.

And then, before we even fully understood what we were doing, we were already in something real.

But if someone asked me what defines our relationship, I wouldn’t say it’s how it started. I’d say it’s what came after.

Distance.

Long distance isn’t romantic. People like to pretend it is, missing each other, emotional messages, but the truth is, it’s hard. It’s frustrating. It tests you in ways you don’t expect.

There are days when you just want the simplest things, sitting next to each other, walking somewhere, doing absolutely nothing together, and you can’t have them. There are moments when a call isn’t enough, when a message feels too small for what you’re feeling. There are misunderstandings that wouldn’t even exist if you were face to face.

And yet, despite all of that, we chose each other.

Again and again.

That’s what I respect the most about us. Not that we stayed together because it was easy, but because it wasn’t.

We didn’t stay out of habit. We stayed because we didn’t want to lose this.

Because staying for seven years isn’t accidental. It’s a choice. A repeated decision to keep going, even when it would’ve been easier not to.

And you made that choice too.

You stayed.

Through my moods, my flaws, my overthinking, my silence, my stubbornness. You stayed through all the versions of me that I’m not proud of. And I know I don’t always say it, but I see it. I notice it. And it matters more than you probably realize.

We didn’t grow up separately, we grew up together.

Seven years means we’ve seen each other change. Different phases, different pressures, different priorities. We’re not the same people we were when we first met.

And yet, somehow, we still fit.

That’s not something I take lightly.

Because a lot of people fall in love at one point in time, but very few manage to keep that connection alive as they evolve. It’s easy to drift apart. It’s easy to become strangers again.

But with you, even when things change, there’s always something that stays the same.

That comfort.
That familiarity.
That feeling of “this is my person.”

You’ve been one of the most constant parts of my life.

In a world where everything keeps shifting, plans, people, situations, you’ve been there. Not perfectly, not without issues, but consistently. And that kind of presence… it’s rare.

And I don’t think we give ourselves enough credit for that.

I also want to be honest about something.

I know I’m not always the easiest person to be with. There are times I don’t express things properly. Times I shut down instead of communicating. Times I overthink or react in ways that don’t make sense. And I know that hasn’t always been fair to you.

But despite all that, you’ve stayed patient more times than I deserved.

And I love you for that.

At the same time, I know we’re not perfect. We’ve both made mistakes. We’ve both said things we shouldn’t have. We’ve both hurt each other at times.

But we never let those moments define us.

We didn’t let one bad day destroy everything we built over years.

Not perfection, but persistence.
Not flawless love, but honest effort.

If I had to describe what you are to me, I would say you're literally my everything.

I don’t know what the future looks like, but I do know one thing.

And that is ki we'll find out together.

I don’t need things to be perfect.

I just need what we’ve always had.

I’m still here.

And I’m still choosing you.

Happy 7 years.
